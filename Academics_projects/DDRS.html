Awareness of Sustainable Development in the Engineering Profession

This document encompasses insights gained from practical exercises aimed at instilling awareness of sustainable development within the engineering profession. Through detailed analysis of carbon emissions associated with computing equipment, Elisa Siuda and Alexis Treilles, guided by their curriculum, evaluated the environmental impact of technology. The exercises included the calculation of CO2 emissions based on the carbon intensity of electrical consumption in France, the exploration of multi-core processing's impact on energy consumption, and comparisons between programming languages and their efficiency in terms of energy consumption.

Through these studies, the students learned about the significance of choosing more energy-efficient computing strategies, such as optimizing software for multi-core processing or selecting programming languages that offer better performance with less energy consumption. The project also delved into the broader implications of hardware choices, comparing the carbon footprint of conventional desktop computers against that of Raspberry Pi clusters coupled with servers, highlighting the potential for substantial reductions in carbon emissions with thoughtful selection of hardware and efficient software design.

By quantitatively assessing the environmental impact of different computing practices and hardware setups, the students gained a nuanced understanding of how engineering decisions can significantly affect sustainability. This project underscores the importance of integrating sustainable development concepts into the engineering profession, encouraging future engineers to consider the environmental consequences of their technical choices.
