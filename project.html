Personal projects

This space is dedicated to what I love doing in my spare time, showcasing projects that might not fit the traditional academic mold but have been instrumental in my self-taught journey across various domains. Each project here is a reflection of my passion and curiosity, approached with a less formal, more exploratory spirit. These endeavors have not only allowed me to amass a vast amount of knowledge in numerous areas but have also honed my ability to learn independently and apply that knowledge creatively. Dive in to see how my interests and self-directed learning come to life outside the classroom.

From where it's begin :

From a young age, I was fascinated by building complex structures from Kapla blocks and creating intricate marble runs. My passion for construction evolved when I discovered my father's 1979 Lego set (Lego 951) at the age of seven. This discovery sparked my creativity, leading me to constantly assemble and reassemble my own Lego sets to create unique inventions. By nine, I had designed a motorized cable car and a mobile tank with a rotating turret that fired rubber bands, followed by a functional tractor model. My interest in engineering further deepened with my first computer, where I spent countless hours exploring the principles of electronics through Minecraft's redstone. At 13, inspired by an online video, I ventured into programming and electronics with my first Arduino board, revealing my father's extensive knowledge in the field. This milestone marked the beginning of an accelerated learning journey, leading to the purchase of my first 3D printer in 2016 and the construction of my first drone in 2017, setting the foundation for my ongoing exploration and innovation in technology.
